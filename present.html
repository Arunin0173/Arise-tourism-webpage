Adventure Tourism Cultural and Heritage Tours Eco-Tourism Luxury Travel Medical Tourism Cruise and Beach Vacations Leisure Tourism Travelers visit destinations for relaxation, entertainment, and recreation. Activities include beach vacations, city tours, and nature exploration. Adventure Tourism Involves activities that require physical exertion and are often thrilling, such as trekking, rafting, and mountain climbing. Cultural Tourism Focuses on exploring cultural landmarks, traditions, and history. Examples include visiting museums, temples, or participating in local festivals. Eco-Tourism Environmentally responsible travel to natural areas, promoting conservation and understanding of local ecosystems. Medical Tourism Travelers seek medical treatments, surgeries, or wellness therapies in another country, often due to lower costs or better healthcare facilities. Business Tourism Includes travel for meetings, conferences, and exhibitions (MICE). Business tourists often combine work and leisure during their trips. Religious Tourism Visiting places of spiritual significance, such as pilgrimage sites ( Mecca, Vatican City, or Varanasi). Sports Tourism Attending or participating in sports events, such as the Olympics or marathons.

Tourism refers to the activities of individuals traveling to and staying in places outside their usual environment for leisure, business, or other purposes. It is one of the largest and fastest-growing industries globally, contributing significantly to economic development, cultural exchange, and the preservation of natural and historical sites.
